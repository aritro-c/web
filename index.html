About Me

I am a computational geoscientist focused on the long-term evolution of terrestrial planets. Currently, I am a Doctoral Student at the Bayerisches Geoinstitut (BGI), supported by a DAAD scholarship. My expertise lies in numerical modeling of planetary interiors, geochemistry, and mantle dynamics.

PhD Research: Venus Evolution

Project: Advanced 3D Numerical Model of Coupled Atmosphere/Mantle Evolution of Venus with Late Accretion Impact Sequences

Using the StagYY thermochemical code, I model the divergent evolution of Venus and Earth. A primary focus is the influence of Late Accretion (LA)—the delivery of meteoric material after core formation—on volatile delivery and planetary habitability.

  • Isotopic Tracers: Using 36Ar and 38Ar to track volatile delivery, and 40Ar to mark volcanic degassing history.
  • 3D Dynamics: Transitioning from 2D models to advanced 3D spherical shell simulations to better represent impact scenarios.
  • Feedback Loops: Investigating the coupling between greenhouse gas degassing (N2, CO2, H2O) and mantle convection.
